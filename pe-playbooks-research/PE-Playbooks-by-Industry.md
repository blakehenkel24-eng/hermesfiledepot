## Cross-Cutting: The Universal PE Value Creation Framework

Before industry specifics, every PE playbook draws from a common set of levers. The mix just changes by sector.

### The 6 Core Levers (per Thomas McCorry / Moonfare / KPMG analysis)
| Lever | What It Does | Speed to Impact |
|-------|-------------|-----------------|
| **Operational Discipline** | KPIs, dashboards, accountability frameworks replace founder gut feel | ~8 months |
| **Financial Structuring** | Optimize capital structure, refinance debt, tighten cash management | Immediate |
| **Professional Management** | Upgrade executives, install boards, succession planning | 3-6 months |
| **Market Expansion** | Bolt-on acquisitions, geographic expansion, product diversification | 6-18 months |
| **Digital Transformation** | ERP integration, data analytics, e-commerce, AI automation | 6-18 months |
| **Pricing Discipline** | Segment customers, test increases — 1% price improvement = double-digit profit boost | **~7.8 months, 4% failure rate** (Simon-Kucher) |

### Key Shift (Bain 2025 / McKinsey 2026)
> "Operational improvement has replaced leverage and multiple expansion as the primary driver of PE value creation. Revenue growth accounted for 71% of value creation in 2024 exits."

- **64% of firms** rank margin growth as top driver
- **63%** prioritize operational efficiency
- **59%** point to digital transformation
- **2/3 of value creation failures** stem from controllable factors (poor implementation, unrealistic business cases, portco resistance)

### The First 100 Days Playbook (Universal)
1. **Stop the bleeding** — Freeze non-essential spend, renegotiate supplier terms, chase invoices
2. **Optimize working capital** — Free up cash immediately
3. **Supply chain triage** — Eliminate sole-source risks, launch sourcing initiatives (target: 8-12% procurement cost reduction)
4. **Re-engineer sales funnel** — Fix drop-off points, implement dynamic pricing
5. **Align incentives** — Tie comp to high-margin products and new customer acquisition

## 1. Technology / Software

### 1A. B2B SaaS
**Confidence: HIGH** — Extensively sourced (Tidemark, Accordion, Intrinsic Search, Simon-Kucher, Octopus Ventures, WSO, academic thesis, Thoma Bravo analysis)

**Entry Thesis:**
- Predictable subscription revenue with high switching costs
- Mission-critical tools with high ARPU and strong upsell potential
- PE interest began ~2012, now at all-time high for SaaS acquisitions

**Value Creation Playbook:**
1. **Cut S&M and R&D spend** — Thoma Bravo's Instructure case: S&M from 45% to 22% of revenue, R&D from 32% to 16%. EBITDA margin went from -28% to +28%
2. **Price increases over customer acquisition** — Revenue growth (34%) outpaced customer growth (17%), indicating strategic pivot to pricing power
3. **Professionalize leadership** — Replace founders with seasoned SaaS operators
4. **Build repeatable GTM engine** — PE expects predictable, data-driven go-to-market
5. **Tech debt cleanup** — Must be future-proof, SOC 2 compliant, AI-ready

**Key KPIs:**
| Metric | Gold Standard | Source |
|--------|--------------|--------|
| Net Revenue Retention | 120%+ (Enterprise), 95%+ (SMB) | Simon-Kucher |
| Gross Margin | 75%+ | Intrinsic Search |
| CAC Payback | <12 months | Octopus Ventures |
| Rule of 40 | Revenue Growth % + EBITDA % ≥ 40 | Simon-Kucher |
| Contribution Margin | ≥30% | Simon-Kucher |

**Notable PE Firms:** Thoma Bravo, Vista Equity Partners, Insight Partners, Genstar Capital, Clearlake, BC Partners

**AI Angle:** "Most companies claiming AI/ML today possess what Vista calls 'aspirational AI'" — Vista's Robert Smith. Real AI shifts software from automating/reporting to predictive/consultative.

**Typical Hold Period:** 3-5 years
**Exit Routes:** Strategic sale (most common), secondary buyout, IPO

### 1B. Vertical SaaS (VSaaS)
**Confidence: MEDIUM-HIGH** — Tidemark playbook + academic thesis

**Entry Thesis:** Fragmented industries where PE has already consolidated the customer base (up to 80% of target market under PE ownership in some industries)

**Unique Value Creation:**
- VSaaS companies become the **standardization layer** across PE portfolios
- Single PE relationship can open doors to dozens of portfolio companies
- PE firms become distribution partners, not just customers

**Academic Insight (Tampere University, 225 European SaaS companies):**
- Vertical SaaS outperforms Horizontal SaaS in balancing growth and profitability (Rule of 40)
- Global customer base significantly boosts both revenue and profitability vs. local
- Majority investments (buyouts) outperform minority investments (growth equity) in both revenue and EBITDA margins

## 2. Healthcare

### 2A. Healthcare Services (General)
**Confidence: HIGH** — Bain 2026 report, PESP, Becker's, Latham & Watkins, McGuireWoods

**Market Context:**
- 2025 was record year: **$191 billion** disclosed deal value (surpassed 2021 record)
- 1,029 PE-backed deals tracked in 2025 (151 buyouts, 664 add-ons, 214 growth investments)
- **Deal discipline has replaced rapid roll-up strategies of prior years**
- Shift from standalone platforms to **integrated physician practice management models**

**Universal Healthcare PE Playbook:**
1. **Platform + MSO model** — Build management services organization around physician practices rather than standalone facilities
2. **Standardize operations** — Centralize billing, HR, IT, procurement across practices
3. **Shift service mix** — Move toward higher-margin procedures (proven in dental: revenue growth from changing mix/intensity of care, not higher per-procedure rates)
4. **Technology stack** — Standardize EHR across portfolio (eClinicalWorks, Epic), implement RCM optimization
5. **De-risk regulatory** — Navigate state and federal compliance (growing complexity, increasing deal costs and timelines)

**AI Angle:** Real-world, measurable AI use cases focusing on drug discovery and optimizing molecule-to-market journey. Investors looking beyond hype.

### 2B. Dental / DSOs
**Confidence: HIGH** — Decisions in Dentistry, Becker's Dental, PESP data

**Market Context:**
- 149 PE-backed dental deals in 2025 (2nd busiest subsector)
- PE involvement began mid-to-late 1990s (Heartland Dental, Smile Brands, Aspen Dental)
- Investment accelerated dramatically 2019-2021
- Now expanding beyond general dentistry into **specialties**: endodontics, pediatric, periodontics, oral surgery

**Entry Thesis:**
- Dentistry is "stable, profitable, and recession proof" (Dr. Barry Lyon, Becker's)
- Highly fragmented market ripe for consolidation
- Strong cash flows with insurance + patient pay mix

**Value Creation Playbook:**
1. **Acquire practices** → standardize operations → achieve economies of scale
2. **Shift service mix** toward higher-margin restorative procedures (away from preventive)
3. **Centralize** billing, scheduling, procurement, marketing
4. **Upgrade technology** — digital imaging, practice management software, patient engagement
5. **Renovate infrastructure** and hire additional staff with PE capital

**Key Finding:** PE acquisition does NOT increase insurer reimbursement rates — revenue growth comes from changing the mix and intensity of care. The long-term risk is market consolidation weakening competition.

## 3. Industrials / Manufacturing

**Confidence: HIGH** — Simon-Kucher PE Study 2025, Carpedia/Centri, Catalant, multiple cross-industry sources

**Market Context:**
- PE in industrials shifting from financial engineering to **operational-first, execution-driven playbook**
- Uncertainty is the defining context (economic downturn 46%, tariffs 41%, geopolitical 41%)
- Operational improvements = **33% of equity story importance** (almost double buy-and-build at 20%)

**Entry Thesis:**
- Fragmented markets with consolidation potential
- Operational inefficiency creates immediate value creation opportunity
- Hard assets provide lender comfort for leverage

**Value Creation Playbook:**
1. **Project Management Office (PMO)** — Structured PMO delivers >$15M annualized savings in first 9 months (Carpedia case study)
2. **Lean manufacturing / Six Sigma** — Process optimization, waste reduction
3. **Digital transformation** — Salesforce CRM, online customer portals, KPI dashboards
   - Case: Legacy B2B industrial parts manufacturer → 5% YoY revenue growth in 6 months, 20% growth in new customers, 50% reduction in order processing time
4. **Supply chain optimization** — Renegotiate 70%+ of contracts, cut discretionary spending 40%
5. **Sales force realignment** — Tie compensation to high-margin products

**AI Angle:** GenAI leveraged in ~1/3 of cases today. Success requires clean data foundation + integrated systems + governance, not "silver bullet" solutions.

**Notable PE Firms:** Clayton Dubilier & Rice, KKR, Blackstone, Apollo, Bain Capital Industrials
